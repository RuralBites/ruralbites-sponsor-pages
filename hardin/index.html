What this looks like in practice

What a RuralBites-supported program looks like on the ground

A program serving meals at a central site knew there were families further out who had stopped coming. Not because they did not need it, but because getting there every week was not realistic.

When delivery started, those families enrolled. Meals were packed the same way, organized into routes, and delivered to households with each stop recorded as part of the program.

"These were the families we always worried about. They were just waiting for a way in."

Just meals that finally reached the door.

How it works for families

Simple for families. Organized for your team.

Families in Big Horn County who cannot consistently reach a site or park pickup can receive meals at home. Here is how it works each week.

1

Family enrolls

Families sign up through the RuralBites app or website. Eligibility is verified. The household is added to the delivery schedule and a confirmation is sent.

2

Meals are prepared and picked up

Your approved vendor delivers pre-packaged 7-day meal boxes to your designated pickup site. Boxes are organized and loaded for delivery.

3

Meals are delivered to the door

Drivers deliver boxes to each household. Every delivery is confirmed and logged. Failed attempts are flagged and documented immediately.

What each box contains

  • Breakfast and lunch components meeting USDA SFSP meal pattern requirements
  • Packaged as a 7-day meal box — one delivery per week covers the full week for each household, keeping operations efficient and manageable at scale
  • Shelf-stable and safe for delivery across rural routes
  • Dietary accommodations coordinated through your vendor — vegetarian and allergen-safe options available

Your service area potential

What a 15 to 30-mile radius from Hardin covers

A 15 to 30-mile delivery radius from a single Hardin pickup site reaches into communities across Big Horn County that have historically had limited access to congregate summer meal sites — including communities across Crow Nation territory.

Communities within reach

  • Crow Agency — approximately 18 miles from Hardin
  • Lodge Grass — approximately 25 miles from Hardin
  • Pryor — approximately 30 miles from Hardin
  • St. Xavier — approximately 28 miles from Hardin
  • Wyola — approximately 30 miles from Hardin
  • Rural Big Horn County households outside existing site coverage

Why Big Horn County is the right place for this

  • One of Montana's highest rates of children qualifying for free and reduced-price meals
  • Large portions of the county are more than 15 miles from any existing SFSP congregate site
  • Many families do not have reliable transportation to reach park pickup consistently
  • Montana OPI has confirmed non-congregate service is available for Summer 2026
