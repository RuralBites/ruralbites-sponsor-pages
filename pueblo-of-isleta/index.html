A practical path to reaching more families across Pueblo of Isleta

Pueblo of Isleta already has the relationships, the mission, and the community trust to serve families across your territory. RuralBites works alongside your team and partners to help make broader reach more manageable by bringing enrollment, delivery coordination, and service support into one place.

The goal is simple: help communities and sponsors increase access for families who count on school year meal support, but may have fewer reliable options once summer begins. With rural delivery now possible, there is an opportunity to help meals reach families closer to home.

Why this matters now

Simple example

What this could look like in practice

Sometimes the clearest way to picture the model is through a simple family scenario.

A practical example

  • A family 20 to 30 minutes from the nearest site signs up using a phone or computer
  • That household is included in a delivery plan for their area
  • Meals are brought closer to where families live through coordinated distribution
  • Service is recorded in a way that supports program requirements and reporting
Possible paths forward

Two paths stood out from our conversation

Both paths are viable. The right direction depends on what Pueblo of Isleta wants to own now and what makes the most sense for Summer 2026.

Path A | Independent Sponsor

Pueblo of Isleta becomes its own SFSP sponsor, giving the community greater long term control over operations and how meals reach families across your territory.

Path B | Partnership Model

Continue working alongside Bernalillo County in the near term, while Isleta leads local coordination and delivery support. This may offer the clearest path for this summer while still creating room to build toward greater independence over time.
